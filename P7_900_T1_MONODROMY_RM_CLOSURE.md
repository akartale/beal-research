## 1. Stable fibre and monodromy form

At t=1 the special fibre of the genus-2 Frey curve is irreducible. Its normalization is the genus-zero curve

    z^2 = 5x^2+5x+1,

and it has exactly two ordinary double points. Over the residue field F_49 of the local base field, both nodes are rational. The geometric dual graph therefore has one vertex and two loop edges e_alpha,e_beta, so

    H_1(Gamma,Z) = Z e_alpha direct_sum Z e_beta.

For a semistable curve, the monodromy pairing on graph homology is the edge-length pairing

    <sum n_e e, sum m_e e> = sum ell_e n_e m_e.

The two nodes arise from the two roots of the same irreducible quadratic factor and have conjugate first-order smoothing coefficients. Their valuations are equal. Hence, in the ordered node-loop basis,

    Q_mon = ell I_2

for a positive integer ell. The scalar ell may be cancelled in the adjointness identity.

## 2. Rosati constraint on real multiplication

Let omega generate the real multiplication order, with

    omega^2-omega-1=0.

    T^t Q_mon = Q_mon T.

Since Q_mon=ell I_2 and ell is nonzero, this implies the integral identity

Thus, after reduction modulo 7, the RM matrix in the node-loop basis is symmetric and satisfies
