# Final quadratic character (90,0): explicit field and Eisenstein obstruction

## 1. Explicit quadratic extension

Let

\[
F=\mathbf Q(\sqrt5),\qquad w=\frac{1+\sqrt5}{2}.
\]

The ray class group for modulus

\[
(3)^3(\sqrt5)^3\infty_1\infty_2
\]

has cyclic structure `[180,2]`. The character `(90,0)` is the order-two character on the first cyclic factor. Its kernel is generated by `2e_1,e_2`, so the corresponding class field is obtained from the subgroup matrix `diag(2,1)`.

The exact PARI/GP script

```text
scripts/identify_900_quadratic_field.gp
```

returns

```text
classfield_polynomials=x^4 - 15*x^2 + 45
```

Over `F`, this polynomial factors through

\[
x^2=\frac{15+3\sqrt5}{2}=3(w+2).
\]

Hence the quadratic character `(90,0)` is the character of

\[
L=F\bigl(\sqrt{3(w+2)}\bigr).
\]

The element `3(w+2)` is positive at both real embeddings, so the character is even at both infinite places. Its norm is `45`, and its finite conductor is exactly

\[
(3)(\sqrt5),
\]

in agreement with the ray-conductor computation.

## 2. Quadratic twist

If

\[
\bar\rho_7^{ss}=\chi\oplus\bar\varepsilon_7\chi
\]

with `chi=(90,0)`, then twisting by `chi` gives

\[
(\bar\rho_7\otimes\chi)^{ss}=1\oplus\bar\varepsilon_7.
\]

Thus the final reducible obstruction is an Eisenstein-type residual representation after quadratic twist.

## 3. Candidate Eisenstein constant term

For the standard parallel-weight-two Eisenstein series over `F` with Euler factors removed at the two primes in the finite conductor, the relevant rational constant-term factor is

\[
\zeta_F(-1)(1-N(3))(1-N(\sqrt5)).
\]

Here

\[
\zeta_F(-1)=\frac1{30},\qquad N(3)=9,\qquad N(\sqrt5)=5.
\]

The exact script

```text
scripts/p7_900_eisenstein_constant_term.gp
```

computes

```text
zeta_F_minus_1=1/30
local_factor_3=-8
local_factor_5=-4
candidate_constant_term=16/15
mod7=Mod(2,7)
```

Therefore this candidate constant term is nonzero modulo `7`.

## 4. Mathematical consequence and remaining theorem bridge

The nonzero constant term is the expected obstruction to a mod-`7` cuspidal congruence with Eisenstein eigenvalues

\[
a_{\mathfrak q}\equiv1+N\mathfrak q\pmod7
\]

after twisting by `chi`.

However, the repository does not yet claim that this numerical calculation alone proves nonexistence of the required cuspidal Hilbert eigenpacket. To make that implication theorem-level complete one must cite and verify an integral Hilbert Eisenstein congruence theorem with exactly the following data:

- base field `F=Q(sqrt5)`;
- parallel weight two;
- trivial residual central character after twist;
- level supported at `(3)(sqrt5)` with the precise stabilization corresponding to the local conductor-two source branch;
- integral normalization of the constant terms at every cusp;
- implication from a cuspidal mod-`7` eigenpacket to divisibility by `7` of the relevant constant-term ideal.

The computed value `16/15` is a `7`-adic unit, so any such theorem with this normalization would eliminate `(90,0)` immediately.

## 5. Exact current status

Mechanically certified:

- explicit quadratic field `F(sqrt(3(w+2)))`;
- finite conductor `(3)(sqrt5)` and even infinite type;
- twisted semisimplification `1 plus bar epsilon_7`;
- candidate Eisenstein constant term `16/15`;
- reduction `16/15 = 2 mod 7`, hence nonzero.

Additional exact computation:

- `P7_900_LEVEL22_BRANDT_CERTIFICATE.md` proves that the target eigensystem is absent from the full mod-`7` Brandt module at level `(2,2)`.

Still theorem-level open:

- a noncircular bridge. Theorem 7.8 assumes irreducibility before applying level lowering to level `(2,2)`, so the Brandt vanishing cannot itself prove irreducibility;
- alternatively, verification of a direct Hilbert Eisenstein/Selmer theorem using precisely the local conditions of this character.

Thus the final character is computationally eliminated at the lowered level, but it is not yet eliminated noncircularly.
