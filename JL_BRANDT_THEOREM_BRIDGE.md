# Jacquet--Langlands/Brandt theorem bridge for the mod-7 eliminations

## Purpose

This document isolates the theorem-level statements needed to convert the exact Brandt-module computations at levels `(2,3)`, `(3,2)`, and `(3,3)` into eliminations of Hilbert newform packets. It deliberately separates proved computational facts from mathematical identifications that still require citation or proof.

Let

\[
F=\mathbf Q(\sqrt5),\qquad \mathcal O_F=\mathbf Z[a],\qquad a^2-a-1=0,
\]

and let \(\mathfrak p_5=(2a-1)\) be the unique prime above `5`. The three computational levels are

\[
\mathfrak N_{23}=(3)^2\mathfrak p_5^3,
\qquad
\mathfrak N_{32}=(3)^3\mathfrak p_5^2,
\qquad
\mathfrak N_{33}=(3)^3\mathfrak p_5^3.
\]

The stored Brandt calculations use the definite Hamilton quaternion algebra over \(F\), the icosian maximal order, and the right action on

\[
\mathbf P^1(\mathcal O_F/\mathfrak N).
\]

The resulting orbit modules have dimensions `226`, `406`, and `2026`, respectively.

## Target theorem

### Theorem JL--Brandt completeness

For \(\mathfrak N\in\{\mathfrak N_{23},\mathfrak N_{32},\mathfrak N_{33}\}\), let \(V_{\mathfrak N}\) be the Brandt module computed by the repository. After fixing the exact parallel weight, central character, and local type used by the Frey representation, there is a Hecke-equivariant identification of the relevant cuspidal newspace with the corresponding quaternionic automorphic space represented by \(V_{\mathfrak N}\). Under this identification:

1. every relevant Hilbert newform system occurs in \(V_{\mathfrak N}\);
2. the operators constructed from norm-prime icosians are the correctly normalized Hecke operators at the labelled prime ideals;
3. oldforms, Eisenstein systems, and multiplicities do not create unrepresented residual eigensystems;
4. reduction modulo a prime above `7` preserves the implication needed by the sieve: a residual Hilbert eigensystem satisfying the Frey trace conditions yields a nonzero simultaneous generalized eigenspace in the computed mod-7 Brandt module.

Once these four assertions are proved, the exact zero-dimensional simultaneous eigenspace calculations recorded for `(2,3)`, `(3,2)`, and `(3,3)` imply that no relevant residual Hilbert newform packet exists at any of these levels.

This theorem is not yet marked proved.

## 2. Quaternion algebra and order

The computational algebra is now explicit.

The scripts use the Hamilton quaternion algebra

\[
B=(-1,-1)_F
 =F\langle i,j\rangle/(i^2=j^2=-1,\ ij=-ji).
\]

Because both real embeddings of \(F\) send `-1` to a negative real number, this algebra is ramified at both real places and is totally definite. The implemented order is the icosian maximal order with the explicit \(\mathcal O_F\)-basis returned by

```text
icosian_order_basis()
```

and norm-one unit group generated by the five elements returned by

```text
icosian_group_generators().
```

The exact closure computation verifies that this norm-one group has order `120`.

At every finite prime dividing one of the three levels, the scripts construct an explicit splitting

\[
B\otimes_F\mathcal O_F/\mathfrak p^e
  \longrightarrow M_2(\mathcal O_F/\mathfrak p^e)
\]

by matrices satisfying

\[
I^2=J^2=-1,\qquad IJ=-JI.
\]

This supplies the concrete local matrix action used in every orbit and Hecke computation.

The exact certificate `ICOSIAN_ORDER_CERTIFICATE.md`, reproduced by `scripts/icosian_order_certificate.py`, verifies multiplication closure of the displayed basis and computes the reduced-trace Gram determinant

\[
\det\bigl(\operatorname{trd}(e_i e_j)\bigr)=a-2,
\qquad N_{F/\mathbf Q}(a-2)=1.
\]

Hence the order discriminant is the unit ideal. It follows that the order is maximal at every finite prime and that \(B\) is split at every finite prime. Since it is ramified at both real places, the complete ramification set is exactly the two real places.

The following facts are therefore fixed:

## 6. Multiplicity and residual generalized eigenspaces

The exact computations use simultaneous generalized eigenspaces over \(\mathbf F_7\), not characteristic-zero diagonalization. This is the correct robust formulation only after proving:

1. each residual Hilbert eigenpacket maps to a nonzero Hecke-stable subquotient of the reduced Brandt module;
2. congruent characteristic-zero packets, non-semisimplicity, and multiplicities still yield a nonzero simultaneous generalized eigenspace;
3. the stacked-kernel rank test detects such a subquotient.

The linear-algebra implication needed is elementary: if a nonzero module admits the specified scalar Hecke action, then it lies in the kernel of a sufficiently high power of each \(T_{\mathfrak q}-a_{\mathfrak q}\). The manuscript must connect the powers used by the scripts to the possible nilpotence indices in the finite module.

## 7. Split-prime labelling and normalization

For every split rational prime used in the sieve, record the two prime ideals explicitly. With \(a^2-a-1=0\), the scripts construct the two operators from conjugate norm-prime elements. The proof must verify that:

- the first matrix is attached to the declared prime \(\mathfrak q\);
- the second is attached to its conjugate \(\bar{\mathfrak q}\);
- the Hecke normalization agrees with the trace normalization of the Frey representation;
- the ordered Frey trace pairs are compared in the same ordering.

This must be checked for `11`, `19`, and `29`. Equality of unordered sets is not enough because the sieve uses ordered pairs.

## 8. Reduction modulo 7

Choose a coefficient ring containing all relevant eigenvalues and a prime \(\lambda\mid7\). The proof must explain why the integral Hecke action admits reduction modulo \(\lambda\) compatible with the matrices over \(\mathbf F_7\) used by the scripts. If an eigenvalue residue field is larger than \(\mathbf F_7\), explain why scalar extension of the computed module preserves the zero-dimensional conclusion.

In particular, if

\[
V\otimes_{\mathbf F_7}k_\lambda
\]

has a nonzero simultaneous generalized eigenspace over \(k_\lambda\), then the corresponding stacked matrix over \(\mathbf F_7\) cannot have full rank. Thus a full-rank certificate over \(\mathbf F_7\) excludes packets over every finite extension.
